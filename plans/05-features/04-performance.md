# Feature: Performance Management

Keep this simple for our ICP. Factory owners and small business managers don't need OKR frameworks — they need a structured way to tell employees how they're doing and have a record of it.

---

## Review Cycles

### Setup (Owner/HR)

- Create a review cycle: name, type (Annual/Quarterly/Probation), date range
- Select rating scale: 1–5 (default), 1–10, custom labels
- Choose who is included (all employees, specific departments, specific employment types)
- Open cycle → employees and managers are notified

### Review Process

1. **Self-review** — Employee rates themselves + comments (optional, configurable)
2. **Manager review** — Manager rates + comments
3. **Calibration** — Owner/HR can override ratings before finalizing (optional)
4. **Share** — Finalized reviews shared with employee (one-way visibility)

### Probation Review

- Automatically triggered 7 days before probation end date
- Manager must complete review before probation end
- Outcome: Confirm employment OR extend probation OR terminate
- Outcome updates employee status automatically

---

## Goals

### Setting Goals

- Manager sets goals for employee (or employee sets + manager approves)
- Goal: title, description, metric/target, due date
- Linked to a review cycle (optional)
- Status: Active, Completed, Cancelled

### Tracking

- Employee updates progress (0–100%) with check-in comments
- Manager can view + comment on progress
- Goals shown on employee profile

### Not Over-Engineering This

- No OKR hierarchies (org > team > individual)
- No complex weighting systems
- Just: set goal → track progress → rate at review time

---

## Continuous Feedback (Phase 2)

- Manager gives kudos or constructive note to employee anytime
- Visible only to employee + their manager chain
- Referenced in review time
- Simple: not a full 360-degree system

---

## Performance Reports

- Review completion rate by department
- Rating distribution (how many 1s, 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s)
- Employees on PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)
- Probation review pending list (urgent!)

---

## PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)

- HR/manager creates PIP for underperforming employee
- Define: goals, timeline, check-in frequency, consequence
- Tracked separately from normal review cycle
- Legal record: important for wrongful termination protection
