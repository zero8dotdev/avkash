Employment Levels

The Ladder

Mahalaxmi doesn't use the old WORKER / EXECUTIVE enum. They defined 10 named levels with a numeric rank — from Director (100) down to Helper Gr. II (5).

Directors and GMs float — their punch routes to wherever they're physically present. This avoids the fiction of a Director "belonging" to one plant.

Operators, Helper Gr. I and II require a supervisor to confirm their WEB punches before they count toward attendance. Device punches bypass this — hardware is trusted; browser is not.

The rank field enables leave policy inheritance — policies can be restricted to rank ≥ 40 (officer and above) with a single config flag.

Three Shifts, One Clock

Factory workers rotate through 3 shifts covering all 24 hours. The General Shift (09:30–18:30) is HQ-only — no overtime tracking for executives.

The C Shift (Night) crosses midnight — 22:00 to 06:00. Avkash marks this with crossesMidnight: true so attendance spans two calendar dates correctly.

A legal restriction applies: no female workers at night in the SEZ plant (Factories Act). The allowedGenders field enforces this at shift assignment time, not punch time.

General Shift has trackOT: false — a policy decision, not a system limitation.

The Overtime Gap

The SEZ labour regime follows the Factories Act (Amendment) 2017, which raises the daily OT threshold from 9 hours to 10 hours.

Avkash reads overtimeThresholdHours directly from the location record — no hardcoded rules. The same resolver handles all regimes.

Result: Deepak at Pune MIDC earns 1.5h OT pay. Anwar at Silvassa earns 0.5h OT pay. Same clock. Different laws.

When a location's regime changes (e.g. new SEZ notification), updating the single regime field re-calibrates all future attendance computations automatically.

On the Move

Ramesh Pawar is transferred to the Silvassa SEZ plant for the new production line ramp-up.

From 2026-06-10, his attendance is computed against the SEZ's 10h OT threshold. The transfer record carries a fromDate — past punches are not retroactively changed.

The system resolves which location applies for a given punch date by walking the transfer history, picking the record whose fromDate ≤ punchDate.

Future-dated transfers are queued and applied by the nightly cron. HR can schedule them in advance during notice periods.

On Probation

Sunita Yadav joined as a Helper Gr. I at Pune MIDC in November 2025. Factory workers must complete 240 days of work (Factories Act) before Earned Leave accrues.

During probation, a policy overlay caps her EL at 0 regardless of what the base leave policy says. The overlay is scoped to employmentStatus = PROBATION.

On June 1, 2026 — after the daily cron runs — she graduates automatically. The cron calls runProbationCompletion(), transitions her status to ACTIVE, and immediately posts 15 days of EL credit.

The policy overlay pattern means zero special-casing in the leave resolver — it reads overlays, base policy, and location in priority order.

Leave Day

Priya Sharma (Corporate White Collar) applies for 3 days Earned Leave — June 15–17. Her manager Prakash Nair gets an in-app notification.

On approval, a TAKEN ledger entry is posted for each day and the balance decrements. All in one atomic transaction — no partial states.

The calendar days flip from blue highlight to green. The balance updates from 10 → 7 with a tick animation.

Leave policies can block applications during blackout periods (quarter-end, audit week). The policy evaluator runs before the ledger write — blocking is clean, not a cancellation.

Half a Day

Kavitha Menon needs the afternoon off for a hospital visit — she'll work the morning. Avkash models this as FIRST_HALF + SECOND_HALF.

The midpoint is computed from the shift's actual start and end — not clock-based MORNING/AFTERNOON labels. A night shift worker's "morning" is at 02:00.

Attempting to apply FIRST_HALF a second time triggers a ConflictError: LEAVE_OVERLAP. The guard runs before any ledger write — the balance is protected.

This model also enables "split" leave days: EL for the morning, Comp-Off for the afternoon — each half tracked independently.
